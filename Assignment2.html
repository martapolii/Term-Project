The Importance of Protected Areas & Strategic Land Use

A protected area is a clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means, to achieve the long term conservation of nature with associated ecosystem services and cultural values." IUCN Definition, 2008

Protected areas and strategic land use are vital towards our effort to safeguard the environment for present and future generations. These areas serve as sanctuaries for biodiversity, preserving our precious ecosystems.

Ecosystems are important becaue they provide essential services, such as purifying our air and water, maintaining biodiversity, and acting as carbon sinks, helping mitigate our changing climate. There are also many societies across the world that depend on their ecosystems for resources to survive, and are under threat due to commercial development, unsustainable harvesting practices, and global warming.

Protected areas help promote sustainable development by balancing conservation with local community needs. They preserve ecosystems, which in turn helps to mitigate climate change, amoung the other numerous services healthy ecosytems provide. Protected areas and land use planning are indispensable for nurturing a healthier, more sustainable planet.
